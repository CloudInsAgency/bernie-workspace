## Key Decisions

**Strategy**
- **Micro-influencers, not macro** — 10K-50K follower range. Higher engagement (5-10%), better authenticity, fits the budget.
- **Flat fee model** ($150/influencer) over commission. Apple's tracking limitations make commission impractical, and the budget doesn't support both.
- **Photo carousel format** specifically — targeting TikTok creators who do visual carousel content with trending sounds. No talking required, works for non-English creators.
- **5-day turnaround** instead of documenting the full 30-day challenge — practical for a first test, lower commitment for the creator.
- **Performance tracking via unique landing page URLs** — `zerosugarclub.com/[creatorname]`, possibly Bitly-shortened.
- **No exaggerated claims** — explicit removal of "15,000+ users" and "1,200+ clubs" copy. Anything unverifiable is out.

**Content**
- **SEO article hits all primary keywords** — sugar free app, quit sugar, no sugar diet, sugar tracker, etc.
- **Science-backed positioning** — peer-reviewed sources only. ASTD 95% accountability stat, Obesity journal social-support data, AHA guidelines (25g women / 36g men daily sugar limits).
- **Article doesn't displace the website** — provides complementary educational content rather than recreating the site's structure.
- **Copyright compliance is strict** — max 15 words per quote, one quote per source, heavy paraphrasing.

**Apple constraints**
- **No custom affiliate tracking via Apple** — codes give discounts, not attribution.
- **Three workarounds explored:** (1) honor system with in-app referral code entry, (2) web-based purchase with redemption code, (3) flat fee only. **Currently going with #3** because it requires zero development.

## Useful Context (positioning + gotchas)

**Product positioning**
- **Accountability clubs** are the primary differentiator. ASTD research shows 95% success rate with structured check-ins; Obesity journal data shows social support produces ~20% better outcomes (per JCCP).
- **One-time $6.99** is a major selling point against the sea of subscription apps.
- **Science-backed, not influencer-hyped** — every health claim should cite a peer-reviewed source. AHA daily sugar limits (25g women, 36g men) are the canonical reference.

**Creator vetting checklist**
- 10K-50K followers (micro sweet spot)
- ≥5-10% engagement rate (lower → likely bots)
- Authentic personal content, not wall-to-wall sponsored posts
- Demographic: 25-45, 60%+ female, health/wellness interest
- Red flags: every post `#ad`, high followers with low engagement, generic comment patterns

**TikTok carousel best practices**
- 6-10 slides (algorithm favors)
- Slide 1 = hook (stop the scroll)
- Mix selfies + screenshots
- 5-10 words per slide, high contrast, don't cover the face
- Last slide = CTA ("Link in bio", "Download now")
- Trending sounds > production polish

**Apple App Store quirks**
- Promo codes give discounts, don't track attribution
- Apple takes 30% of paid app/IAP revenue
- External payment redirects to bypass 30% are against TOS — risks app removal
- Workaround: sell on website, deliver redemption code (gray area, dev work needed)

**Things to avoid**
- "15,000+ members" or "1,200+ clubs" — removed, do not reintroduce
- Specific weight-loss promises — FTC compliance issue
- More than 15 words from any single source in articles — copyright
- 100% upfront payment — protect against ghosting with 50/50 split
- Skipping FTC disclosure (`#ad`, `#partner`) — legally required
- Macro/celebrity influencers — outside budget, lower engagement, worse fit

**Craig's preferences for this project**
- Concise, actionable deliverables (brief > essay)
- Free / low-cost tools (Bitly, Google Sheets, Vercel, Firebase free tiers)
- Systematic tracking — spreadsheets, metrics, ROI calculations
- Evidence-based decisions — cite research, show data, avoid hype
